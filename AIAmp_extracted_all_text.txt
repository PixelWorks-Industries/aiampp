Insights & Case studies

AI amplifies who you already are.

Intelligence is not judgment. We are letting agentic AI making decisions before teaching it what matters most: good human judgment. Sometimes it borrows from a prompt we typed in a hurry. Sometimes from the average of the internet. Sometimes from the company that built the system long before we showed up. The problem is that we rarely know whose judgment it is borrowing, and a generation that already trusts almost nothing is now being asked to trust decisions no one can explain. The companies that win keep three controls human, then wire three gears of AI behind them. Here is the approach, and the proof.

THE AMPLIFIER RULE

Amplified value — three controls present. Friction and uncertainty removed, trust delivered, at scale.

Amplified noise — controls missing. Expensive, confident-sounding mistakes.

WHY THIS MOMENT IS DIFFERENT · THREE CONTROLS MUST STAY HUMAN

Otherwise, the model will invent — with confidence.

The Steering — Deep customer understanding. Knowing where the journey breaks, where doubt accumulates, and where the customer stops believing. AI cannot earn the right to act without it.

The Gearbox — Responsible AI framework. Guardrails that make every action fair, explainable and reversible. Not the brake — the gearbox that lets you scale safely.

The Driver — Human leadership. The courage to leave the industry script, sign up for the promise, and own the outcome. Judgment stays with people.

WHEN THE CONTROLS ARE MISSING

Your ghost Formula One car with an empty seat is ready to crash your brand.

Negative example · The steering missing — IBM Watson Health. IBM Watson Health had powerful AI but weak steering — it did not sufficiently reflect how clinicians actually make decisions. Intelligence, without the trust of the people whose judgment mattered most. $4B invested, mostly written off, some parts sold.

Negative example · Gearbox & driver missing — Air Canada. Air Canada’s chatbot invented a refund promise the company never intended to make — weak gearbox, weak driver: not enough controls, accountability, or reversibility. The court saw what customers already knew: when the AI speaks to the customer, the brand is speaking. The tribunal ruling itself was small (~CAD$812). The lasting damage was the precedent and the brand hit, not the cash settlement.

THE STEERING · BEFORE THE MODEL, THE MOMENT

Find where the customer slows down, hesitates, abandons, or stops believing.

That is where AI earns the right to act.

Friction — The signal — where the journey breaks. Process makes the customer work harder than they should. Steps repeat. Forms multiply. Energy drains.

Uncertainty — The doubt — where doubt accumulates. The customer cannot predict what happens next. The price might change. The promise might bend.

Trust — The belief — where AI earns belief. The customer wonders whether the business acts in their interest. Trust must be designed, not declared.

THE THREE GEARS OF AI

Once the human moment is clear, wire three gears in sequence — and turn a transaction into a relationship.

01 · Perceptive AI — SEE. Detects live friction. Vision, voice, behavior, language. Senses the signals the customer cannot easily explain.

02 · Analytical AI — KNOW. Reads uncertainty. Connects the signal to value, risk, and timing. Decides what the moment means.

03 · Generative AI — DO. Acts on trust. Executes the promise, explains the choice, reassures at the moment of truth.

THE GEARBOX · RESPONSIBLE AI

Responsible AI is not the brake. It is the gearbox.

Make it easier — without taking control.

Provide clarity — without false certainty.

Make decisions fair, explainable, reversible.

“Irresistibly alluring today. A force unto itself tomorrow. Scale trust before you scale tech.” — Noelle Russell, Scaling Responsible AI (Wiley, 2025)

THE PROOF · SAME PATTERN, DIFFERENT INDUSTRY

Two businesses that kept the controls human — and amplified the value.

Retail · Bridal · The Pearl app — David’s Bridal — 1 → 18-month relationship · NPS 19 → 73 · $4B dresses → $65B full-journey market.

Telecom · UK device trade-in — Vodafone UK Trade-In — 5× trade-in volume · NPS +32 over industry. From a painful trade-in to a price guarantee.

Cybersecurity · NA market entry — European Cyber Security Company — 6 weeks · Board-ready recommendation. High-stakes North America validation.

Winners have the courage to see the customer differently — despite industry rules — and deploy all three gears of AI with the right human controls.

RESOLUTION & DELIVERABLES

Ten conversations with the people who actually decide. One framework the board could underwrite.

Double-digit $M — Year-5 growth potential, bottom-up. Growth potential sized bottom-up across 55 mobile and 41 fixed operators; company-capturable revenue modeled at the operator level, with attach, pricing and rev-share assumptions stress-tested.

Executive-level interviews — Heads of product, security and VAS at Tier-1 and Tier-2 operators across Canada and the US — plus an independent advisor. Not analysts. The people who own the purchase order.

3 × 90 days — Conditional commitment framework. Three explicit gates — RFP engagement, partner LOI, US compliance readiness. Each one tested before the next dollar leaves the board.

"Ten conversations" in body vs. "5 executive-level operator interviews" in stats. Conflict.

THE DIAGNOSTIC UNDERNEATH

Independent judgment, free of internal bias — and a US-grade plan.

Key buying factors — heat-mapped — 13 KBFs tested against five primary interviews and a competitive bench. The result split three ways — and two of those ways the client had not seen.

Aligned with self-assessment — Buying factors where the client’s own read matched the operator evidence.

Emerging moats — Advantages the client did not yet know they owned — now part of the positioning.

MEASURABLE OUTCOMES

A position, not a menu. Conditional on tests the board can read.

Recommendation delivered — Canada-direct + US-via-partner hybrid · staged $5–7M envelope · breakeven Y4.

Bottom-up TAM / SAM / SOM at the operator level · KBF heat-map across 13 buying factors · Model A / B / C deployment analysis (network DNS · cloud DNS · app-SDK) · 5-year EBITDA case with peak-invest envelope · partner motivation scorecard · 90-day gate framework with explicit pass / fail criteria.
